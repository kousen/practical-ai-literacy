# Lab 6 (Homework): Evaluate a Tool Yourself

**Time:** ~30 minutes, on your own, with a tool of your choice

This is the durable skill from the workshop: the tools will change; your
evaluation method shouldn't.

## Pick a tool you haven't seriously used

Ideas: a chat tool from a different vendor than your daily one, Copilot in an
app you haven't tried, NotebookLM, a voice tool like Wispr Flow's trial, or —
if you're feeling ambitious — an agentic tool like Claude Code or Codex in
ChatGPT.

## The evaluation method

1. **Give it a task you already know the answer to.** Something from your real
   work where you can judge the output instantly — last month's report, a
   process you know cold, a spreadsheet you built.

2. **Push until it fails.** Make the task progressively harder: more data,
   more constraints, more ambiguity. Every tool has a boundary. Finding it is
   the whole exercise.

3. **Write down three sentences:**
   - What it did better than you expected
   - Where it broke, and how obvious the breakage was
   - One task from your job you'd actually hand it

## Share it

Bring your three sentences to an AI Lab session at the Elting Center — or just
share them with your team. Ten people doing this once a quarter is a genuinely
good institutional radar for what these tools can do now.

## Why this works

Vendor demos show you the happy path. Benchmarks measure tasks that aren't
yours. But a task where *you* know ground truth, pushed until it breaks, tells
you exactly what this tool is worth for your work — today. Repeat when the
next version ships.
